Research

Since August 2016 I have been studying to get my Master's degree in Physics at University of São Paulo under advisance of Prof. Nestor Caticha .

My research deals with models of bayesian agents to study collective phenomena in societies. With a bit less jargon, we use Information Theory, Machine Learning and Statistical Mechanics to model agents that, in a certain way, simulate human behavior, social interaction and moral reasoning.

The study consists of two steps: the agent and the society

  1. Agent: experimental results from Neuroscience and from Moral Psychology constrain and guide our agent modelling. From what we know about human (individual) behavior we develop "architectures" that capture some relevant characteristic. From this we study the consequences of the agent model, how it interacts with the information delivered to it, how ir reacts, learns and emits "signals";
  2. Society: fixing the behavior of an agent, we can construct models of societies of agents. The question now enters a macroscopic domain, that is, which kinds of global (statistical) characteristics we discover in a society model when it is built from specific types of agents? From that we can make predictions and compare with data on past/modern societies.
